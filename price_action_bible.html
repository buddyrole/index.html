// SECTION 01

THE THREE CANDLE TYPES

STRENGTH

Strength Candle

Large body, small or no wicks. One side dominated the entire session with little to no contest. The bigger the body, the stronger the conviction.

"We went this direction and nobody stopped us."
CONTROL SHIFT

Control Shift Candle

Small body, long wick on ONE side. One side attempted a move and the opposing side took over before close. The long wick is the failed attempt.

"They tried hard. We won."
INDECISION

Indecision Candle

Small body, wicks on BOTH sides. Price went up AND down and closed near where it opened. Neither side could dominate. No edge here.

"Nobody won. Wait."
!

The Universal Truth

Strength candles = stay with the trend. Control shift candles at key levels = watch for reversal. Indecision candles = wait for direction. Location determines which one actually matters.

!

Speed Matters Too

A strong move that took 1 hour and a pullback that took 4 days = buyers still winning the war. The velocity of a move relative to the opposing move reveals institutional intent.

// SECTION 02

LOCATION RULES

"THE COLOR OF THE CANDLE DOES NOT MATTER.
WHAT MATTERS IS WHERE IT APPEARS AND HOW IT'S FORMED."

— THE FOUNDATIONAL RULE OF CANDLESTICK READING
BOTTOM OF DOWNTREND

Bullish Location

Any reversal candle forming at the bottom of a clear downtrend at a key support level signals buyer defense regardless of candle color.

Wick pointing down at the bottom = buyers defending lower prices
TOP OF UPTREND

Bearish Location

Any reversal candle forming at the top of a clear uptrend at a key resistance level signals seller pressure regardless of candle color.

Wick pointing up at the top = sellers rejecting higher prices
MID-RANGE

No Man's Land

Any reversal candle forming in the middle of a range or sideways market is meaningless noise. Ignore it. No level = no context = no edge.

Mid-range candles of any shape have zero directional edge
2x

The 2X Wick Rule — Mandatory Filter

The wick must be at least 2x the size of the candle body or the pattern is disqualified. A small wick means price tested and got a slight rejection. A 2x+ wick means one side got aggressively pushed back. That's conviction. Anything less is a shrug.

THE HAMMER FAMILY

Four patterns. Two shapes. One rule: location determines bias, not color.

BULLISH
Hammer
Bottom of downtrend
BEARISH
Hanging Man
Top of uptrend
BEARISH
Shooting Star
Top of uptrend
BULLISH
Inverted Hammer
Bottom of downtrend

BOTTOM = BULLISH

Hammer · Inverted Hammer

Long lower wick at bottom = buyers rejected lower prices. Long upper wick at bottom = buyers probing higher. Both show buyer presence at support.

TOP = BEARISH

Hanging Man · Shooting Star

Long lower wick at top = sellers warning during the candle. Long upper wick at top = sellers rejecting the push higher. Both show seller presence at resistance.

→

Formation = Alert. Confirmation = Entry.

The candle forming at the level is the soldier raising his hand saying "I'm here." The confirmation candle is that soldier actually holding the line. Never enter on the pattern alone — wait for the next candle to follow through in the reversal direction.

// SECTION 04

THE DOJI FAMILY

All Dojis = indecision. The specific type tells you how intense the battle was. Location tells you whether it matters.

Standard Doji

Equal wicks. Pure indecision. Neither side had an edge.

Long-Legged Doji

Very long wicks both sides. Extreme battle. Nobody won. Big move coming.

Dragonfly Doji

Long lower wick, no upper. Bears tried. Bulls dominated. Bullish at support.

Gravestone Doji

Long upper wick, no lower. Bulls tried. Bears dominated. Bearish at resistance.

Doji Star

Doji after strong candle. Trend pausing. Wait for confirmation candle.

POWER SIGNAL

Double Doji = Compression

Two consecutive Dojis = volatility coiling like a spring. When it breaks, the move is fast and decisive. On SPY intraday — this is your pre-explosion warning. Get ready.

DISTINGUISH

Doji vs Spinning Top

Doji: Open = Close. Complete equilibrium. Stronger reversal signal when confirmed.

Spinning Top: Small but visible body. Mild indecision. Sign of hesitation, not reversal.

⚠ TIMEFRAME WARNING — CRITICAL FOR SPY 0DTE On 15M / 5M / 1M charts, Doji signals are LOW reliability — often noise. Only valid with confluence: VWAP positioning + volume spike + key level. A Doji alone on a 1M chart means nothing without location context.

NEW EDGE FILTERS

These two filters are your toolbox upgrades. Apply them after LTProv7 fires — before entry.

FILTER 01 — SPEED / VELOCITY CHECK

Before every entry, ask one question:

"Is the pullback SLOWER and CHOPPIER than the impulse that preceded it?"
✓ YES — TRADE IT Fast impulse + slow choppy pullback = trend intact. The dominant side is still in control. Pullback entry is valid.
✗ NO — STAND DOWN Pullback matching impulse speed = trend weakening. The opposing side is gaining. Avoid or flip bias.
This is institutional footprint detection. Fast up + slow down = smart money still positioned long. Speed disparity reveals who's really in control.
FILTER 02 — WICK vs BODY CLOSE AT LEVELS

When price hits your ZONE or LEVEL, read the candle closes:

"Are we seeing wicks only — or are bodies closing beyond the level?"
✓ WICKS ONLY — A+ SETUP No body closes beyond the level. The opposing side is actively defending. Level is holding. This is your highest conviction entry context.
✗ BODY CLOSES THROUGH — STEP ASIDE Bodies closing beyond the level = level is failing. The defending side is losing. Do not fight this. Let it go.
