⚡ Quick Candle Reference — Bodies vs Wicks Rule
BULLISH signals (bodies matter):
✅ Green body CLOSING above resistance
✅ Lower wick = buyers defended support
✅ Body above previous candle's high
❌ Green wick only = NOT confirmed
❌ Small body inside range = indecision
BEARISH signals (bodies matter):
✅ Red body CLOSING below support
✅ Upper wick = sellers defended resistance
✅ Body below previous candle's low
❌ Red wick only = NOT confirmed
❌ Small body inside range = indecision
THE GOLDEN RULE: Wicks lie. Bodies confirm. Always wait for the CLOSE.

4-Hour Candle Box Strategy
📦 The 4H Box — Core Concept
Use the previous 4H candle to create your box. The next candle is what you trade. London session 4H candle (4AM–8AM EST) sets your battlefield for the NY session.

How to draw: Press R on TradingView → Click HIGH → Drag to LOW. Right-click → Lock when done.

Rule: Only use candles with BIG BODY (body > wick). Avoid skinny/indecision candles.

🎯 The 4 Zones Inside the Box
🟠 0–25% — PREMATURE ZONE
Top of the box. Too early — entry can be faked out. Price may still consolidate here. Wait for deeper confirmation.
🟢 25–50% — OPTIMUM ZONE ⭐ (Best Entry)
Sweet spot. Momentum confirmed, room to run. This is where you want to enter. Deep enough retracement, not too deep.
🟡 50–75% — OVEREXTENDED ZONE
Getting late. Market is 50/50 between buyers and sellers. Reduced reward, increased risk. Avoid if possible.
🔴 75–100% — DANGER ZONE (No Entry)
Price is disrespecting the previous impulse. DO NOT enter here. Price may reverse entirely. Wait for next setup.

Lit Exchange = Public market everyone sees on TradingView. Every trade visible in real time.

Dark Pool = Private exchange where institutions trade large blocks WITHOUT showing their hand. By the time it's reported, they're already positioned.

What to watch: When dark pool volume SPIKES above average = institutions moving size = big directional move coming.

📈 Open Interest — Call & Put Walls
Call Wall = Strike with massive call open interest. Institutions DEFEND this level (their calls need price above it). Acts as SUPPORT.

Put Wall = Strike with massive put open interest. Acts as RESISTANCE. Institutions profit if price stays below.

When price approaches a call wall from ABOVE = institutional battle. They fight to hold it. Break below = they stop defending = next level activates fast.

📋 ForexBee: The Strat Patterns — Complete Guide
📖 Definition

The Strat patterns are a set of specific price bar sequences used to identify trend directions, potential market reversals, and trade entry points in a multi-timeframe trading approach.

Rob Smith developed the strat patterns trading strategy using price action. He uses the concept of broadening and contracting nature. Because waves contract and expand, it is a natural process. He applied this method in trading and made a very high-probability trade setup using multiple timeframes and other confluences.

Broadening: Expanding market — price will make a bullish or bearish trend.

Contraction: Price moves inward toward a point. Each progressive wave is smaller than the previous one.
🔢 Three Candle Types (Foundation of All Patterns)
#1 Inside Bar
Forms within the range of the previous candle. Signal of indecision in the market. Market holding breath before move.
#2 Trending Bar
Shows bullish or bearish trend. Breaks high or low of previous candle. Must have big body compared to wicks. Market choosing a direction.
#3 Outside Bar

🌊 ForexBee: S&P 500 — 5th Wave Strategy
📖 Definition

The 5th wave in trading is the final and longest wave in a trend that typically emerges at the end of a price trend and forecasts a trend reversal. It is characterized by the formation of higher highs and lower lows, often resulting in a stop loss hunting pattern.

This wave signifies the potential end of the current trend and the beginning of a new direction in the market. Markets tend to respect the number 5 more than any other Fibonacci number — that's why this wave is so important.

📐 Fibonacci Wave Sequence
Each wave in the broadening pattern is related to the Fibonacci sequence and tends to be 1.618× longer than the previous wave. Markets turn by forming waves based on Fibonacci numbers: 3, 5, and 8.
⬇️ Bearish 5th Wave
Occurs at the end of a bullish trend. Broadening pattern forms after upward trend. The 5th wave that follows is typically bearish — signals reversal from bullish to bearish.

Wave Counting (Bullish Prior Trend):

  • 1️⃣
    Wave 1: Small bearish wave down
  • 2️⃣
    Wave 2: Bullish bounce — larger than W1, makes higher high
  • 3️⃣
    Wave 3: Bearish — bigger than W2, makes lower low
  • 4️⃣
    Wave 4: Bullish — new higher high, larger than W3. Use Fib on W3 to find end.
  • 5️⃣
    Wave 5: FINAL — larger than all previous waves. Entry here.
⬆️ Bullish 5th Wave
Occurs at the end of a bearish trend. Broadening pattern forms after downward trend. The 5th wave that follows is typically bullish — signals reversal from bearish to bullish.

Wave Counting (Bearish Prior Trend):

  • 1️⃣
    Wave 1: Small bullish wave up
  • 2️⃣
    Wave 2: Bearish — larger than W1, makes lower low
  • 3️⃣
    Wave 3: Bullish — bigger than W2, creates higher high
  • 4️⃣
    Wave 4: Bearish — goes lower than W3 but larger in size. Use Fib on W3.
  • 5️⃣
    Wave 5: FINAL — exceeds all previous waves. Bullish entry zone.
🎯 How to Trade the 5th Wave in S&P 500
  • 1️⃣

  • 2️⃣

  • 4️⃣

⚠️ Why the 5th Wave Works (Market Maker Context)

The 5th wave pattern is used by market makers for stop loss hunting. At the end of a trend, price makes higher highs and lower lows deliberately to create more liquidity.

Stop Loss Hunting Cycle:
1. Price hits higher highs → attracts more buyers
2. Price drops to lower lows → brings in sellers, shakes out buyers
3. Price goes up again (higher high) → pulls in buyers, pushes out sellers
4. Market makers have built the liquidity they need → 5th wave launches

This cycle of higher highs and lower lows WITHOUT a clear trend is deliberate stop loss hunting. Most often this happens on the 5th wave, in line with the Fibonacci sequence.
