Signal Logic
The rules and hierarchy behind every signal in the system.
RULE #1 — ABSOLUTE
SIGNAL = WAIT means hands off
No matter how bullish the environment. No matter how strong the volume. No matter what BIAS, VWAP, or QQQ say. If SIGNAL says WAIT, you do not enter. This single rule will save you more money than any other.
RULE #2 — ABSOLUTE
APEX fires first. Signal fires second.
APEX = preparation. It tells you the coil is loaded. APEX is NOT an entry signal. Wait for the SIGNAL row to show BUY CALL or BUY PUT after APEX fires. The direction is unknown at APEX.
RULE #3 — CRITICAL
Confluence = decision point, not direction
When multiple signals align — BIAS, VWAP, Zone, Volume — this tells you a decision point is near. It does not tell you which direction. You still need SIGNAL to confirm the entry.
RULE #4 — CRITICAL
VOL TRAP = stand down
FAT volume with no price expansion = smart money hiding direction. Do not force a trade during VOL TRAP. Wait for the trap to resolve — price will either break out or reverse. Let it show you.
RULE #5
AT LEVEL + AT ZONE = highest probability
A signal that fires AT a mapped level AND at a zone (especially CONFLUENCE) is the best entry the system can give you. This is where you use maximum size. Mid-range signals with no zone = minimum size or skip.
RULE #6
SKINNY volume = no trade
No volume = no institutional participation = no follow-through. SKINNY volume on any breakout signal reduces conviction to zero. The signal might be technically valid, but without participation it won't move. Skip or use minimum size.
RULE #7
Sweep then Fail = the trade
The core of the LT system: price sweeps above equal highs or below equal lows (liquidity grab), then fails back inside the range. That failure is the entry. SWEEP + FAIL LONG = calls. SWEEP + FAIL SHORT = puts.
RULE #8
Environment confirms, signal executes
QQQ + VIX + ES1! tell you the playing field. They confirm but do not create entries. All three green = trust your call signals. Mixed = reduce size and require more confirmation. All three red = only take put signals.

Glossary
Every term used in LTProv7 — plain language definitions.
ORB (Opening Range Breakout)
The high and low of the first 15 minutes of RTH. Most reliable intraday breakout levels.
RTH (Regular Trading Hours)
9:30am – 4:00pm ET. The main session. ORB and entry signals only fire during RTH by default.
VWAP
Volume Weighted Average Price. Institutional fair value. Above = buyers in control. Below = sellers.
EMA20 / EMA50
20 and 50 period Exponential Moving Averages. EMA20 > EMA50 = bullish cloud. EMA20 < EMA50 = bearish cloud.
Liquidity Sweep
Price spikes above equal highs or below equal lows to grab stop orders, then reverses. The core LT setup.
Equal Highs / Equal Lows
Two or more pivot highs/lows at approximately the same price. These cluster stop orders above/below = liquidity pool.
Sweep Fail / Reversal
After a sweep, price fails to hold outside the range and reverses back inside. This is the entry signal.
Range Box
The gray box drawn between equal highs and equal lows. The consolidation zone the system is watching.
ATR (Average True Range)
14-period measure of average price movement. Used for stop placement (1.5x ATR), zone detection, and compression thresholds.
Triangle Apex
When ascending and descending trendlines of a triangle cross. Breakout is imminent but direction unknown.
Measured Move
The projected price target after a triangle breakout = triangle height added to the breakout point. Shown as 🎯 TARGET on chart.
Fakeout
A false breakout — price breaks the triangle trendline then reverses back inside within 3 bars. Do not enter. Wait for retest.
FAT Volume
Volume 1.5–2.5x the 20-bar average. Solid institutional participation. Trade normally.
VERY FAT Volume
Volume 2.5x+ the 20-bar average. Strong institutional participation. At breakout = maximum conviction.
SKINNY Volume
Volume below 1.5x average. Low participation. Skip signals or use minimum size.
VOL TRAP
FAT volume + small body + small range + not at breakout. Effort vs Result mismatch. Smart money hiding direction.
Effort vs Result
Trading concept: high volume (effort) should produce price movement (result). When it doesn't, institutions are absorbing orders.
Absorption
Large orders being filled without moving price. Seen as high volume + small candle. Precedes explosive moves.
Confluence Zone
Weekly AND daily S&R zones stacked within ATR distance. Highest probability location in the system.
Dapo Method
S&R zone methodology using prior weekly/daily highs and lows as institutional reference levels. Used for zone detection.
Marubozu
Candle with body ≥ 85% of range. Virtually no wicks. Pure directional conviction. Strongest candle type.
Doji
Candle body ≤ 10% of range. Open and close nearly equal. Pure indecision. At key level = decision point ahead.
Hammer
Small body near top, long lower wick ≥ 2x body. Sellers pushed price down but buyers rejected it. Bullish reversal.
Shooting Star
Small body near bottom, long upper wick ≥ 2x body. Buyers pushed price up but sellers rejected it. Bearish reversal.
Engulfing
Current candle body completely engulfs prior candle body. Shows complete directional takeover.
Pine Pullback
Price pulls back to EMA20, then reclaims (call) or rejects (put) it while bias and RSI confirm. Secondary entry setup.
Flatten
Close all positions at 3:55pm. Mandatory end-of-day risk management rule built into the system.
ES1!
S&P 500 E-mini futures continuous contract. Leads cash SPY. Used for overnight bias and environment reading.
VIX
CBOE Volatility Index. Fear gauge. VIX up = fear rising = bearish for SPY. VIX down = fear dropping = bullish for SPY.
QQQ
Nasdaq 100 ETF. Tech sector proxy. Moves with SPY. Both green = risk-on environment confirmed.
